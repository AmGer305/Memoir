Project Overview

A memoir about brilliance and boundaries, the South and the North, rockets and setbacks — and what we pass on to the next generation.

Overall Progress Phase 1 — Interviews
1949 — Beaumont, Texas
Childhood in the Segregated South
The second of five children. Different fathers, one mother: Gloria. A gifted boy in a world that promised him very little.
c. 1961 — Washington, D.C.
Uncle Mitch & Aunt Mabel
The gifted boy is sent by Gloria to live with her brother. Out of the South, into a new chance. Howard University awaits.
1960s — Howard University
Physics, Fatherhood, Civil Rights
A father at 17. Repairing TVs to make ends meet. A degree in physics. The world is changing — and so is he.
1981–1988 — NASA
Programming the Space Shuttle
Part of the team that programmed the first Space Shuttle. Then: racist structures that even rocket science can't overcome. He walks away.
1988+ — Germany
New Country, New Love
His own company brings him to Germany. 1994: Sharon. 2000: Christine. A family, a new home — until everything falls apart.
2003–2006 — The Break
Job Loss, Depression, Starting Over
2003: the job disappears. 2005: the lowest point. The divorce. Sharon chooses to stay with her father — then gets "sent away" herself.
Today
Father and Daughter Tell the Story
Two voices, one story. Making the echo between generations heard.

Workflow & Timeline

How we work together — step by step, at your pace.

Phase 1 — Chronology Active
The Big Timeline

The first calls are for free-form storytelling. You tell me your life chronologically, from Beaumont to now — I listen, take notes, and only interrupt to ask follow-ups. The goal: a scaffold of facts, places, people, and finding where your energy lives — which episodes you linger on, which ones you skip.

  • 4–6 calls, 90 minutes each
  • Every conversation recorded and transcribed
  • After each session: summary + open questions for next time
Phase 2 — Deep Dives Upcoming
The Key Moments

Now we go back to the moments that matter and dig in. Sensory details, dialogue, contradictions. Not what happened — but what it felt like. Ideally: a weekend, face to face, at a hotel. Intensive and focused.

  • 3–4 deep-dive sessions per life chapter
  • Photos, documents, artifacts as memory triggers
  • In-person sessions for the difficult material
Phase 3 — The Hard Stuff Upcoming
Depression, Loss, the Fractures

This takes trust and timing. Not in the first session, but not put off forever either. You set the pace. Anything you don't want in the book stays out — but let's at least talk about it, so I understand the full picture.

  • Sensitive topics only with explicit consent
  • You have veto power over any content, at any time
  • These conversations ideally happen in person
Phase 4 — Reflection & Review Upcoming
What Should Remain?

The final interviews focus on meaning: What did you learn? What do you want to pass on? In parallel, you read the first chapters — your corrections, your veto, your blessing.

  • Manuscript chapters available for review in the "Manuscript" tab
  • Space for comments, corrections, and additions
  • Regular check-ins on how you're feeling about the process
Session Format
Structure of Every Interview Call
  • 0–5 min: Settle in, ritual question: "What's come back to you?"
  • 5–65 min: Interview — storytelling, follow-ups, details
  • 65–80 min: Free recall — the best stuff often surfaces at the end
  • 80–90 min: Debrief — what's next, loose threads, open questions

Tools & Tech Stack

The software we use — keeping it simple, reliable, and secure.

Zoom
Video calls with local recording. We use local recording (not cloud) so everything stays on your machine and mine.
Interviews
OBS Studio
Backup screen and audio recording. If Zoom messes up the recording, we have a safety net.
Backup
Whisper (OpenAI)
Automatic transcription. Runs locally on my machine — your words never leave the computer. Supports English and German.
Transcription
Scrivener
My writing software for the manuscript. Chapters are exported as HTML and made visible for you in the Manuscript tab.
Manuscript
Google Drive / Dropbox
Cloud backup for all recordings, transcripts, and manuscript versions. Shared folder we both have access to.
Backup & Sync
This Website
Our project hub. Questions, schedule, manuscript — all in one place. No accounts, no logins. One .html file that runs anywhere.
Organization
Recording Checklist
Before Every Call
  • Start Zoom, enable local recording
  • Run OBS as backup (screen + system audio)
  • Use headphones with a microphone — not the laptop mic
  • Glass of water ready, phone on silent
  • Have this site's Questions tab open
  • After the call: save recording → cloud backup → start transcription
